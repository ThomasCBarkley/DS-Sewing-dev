This foolproof method relies on the same principles used by land surveyors. It may seem a little tricky at first, but most people have no problem with it. 1. Find the shape that best represents your pool: rectangular, oval, round, kidney shaped, or �L�-shaped etc....
 
 

2. If you do not have a round or kidney-shaped pool, identify each point on your pool�s perimeter where two lines intersect. �Points of intersection� include the corners of the pool and the corners of your stairwell if it is external. For an oval pool, the points of intersection are where the parallel lines on either side meet the arcs at each end. If you have a diving board, the back corners are considered points of intersection, as well as the places on the pool�s perimeter which are directly underneath either side of the board. If your pool has a ladder, the places where the railings connect to the sidewalk are points of intersection, and so are the areas on the pool perimeter directly underneath each railing. (pictures of intersecting points on rectangular, oval, and �L�-shaped pools � each with external stairs, a ladder, and a diving board)

                                                                                                         
* Note: Most pools have rounded corners, so to find the true �points of intersection� you must take a ruler and extrapolate where the sides would have met if the corner was a perfect 90 degree angle.

3. Place a small, triangle-shaped piece of masking tape at each point of intersection. Draw a dot on the tape at the exact place where the lines meet, and number each piece of tape in order as you go around the pool�s perimeter. (picture of pool perimeter showing pieces of tape with dots and numbers) For oval pools, in addition to placing tape where the parallel lines meet the arcs at either end, put pieces of tape in a few places around each arc � including the tops of the arcs. (picture showing an arc with pieces of tape around it) For a round pool, place eight triangular pieces of tape at evenly spaced intervals around the perimeter. (picture showing where tape pieces go around round pool) For a kidney-shaped pool, place a triangular piece of tape at the top of each arc and at several other places in between. (picture showing where to put tape around kidney-shaped pool) Label the location and number of each triangle on the pool outline located on D.S. Sewing�s printable pool-measuring form.
 
4. Draw a dot in the middle of two small square-shaped pieces of tape. Label one square �1� and the other �2.� Attach the pieces to the ground somewhere near your pool, spaced so that the dot on square one is exactly 10 feet away from the dot on square two.
 5. Using a long tape measure, find the distance from the dot on square one to the dot on triangle one. Then measure the distance from square two to triangle one. Record these measurements in the appropriate chart on your printable form. Repeat this process with every piece of tape around the pool, measuring the distance from each triangle to squares one and two.
 6. Measure the distance from the dot on triangle one to the dot on triangle two, then from triangle two to triangle three, and so on. Record these numbers in the appropriate chart on your printable form.
 
7. Measure the distance across the pool between triangles, wherever possible. Record these numbers on your printable form.(picture showing lines connecting triangles across pool)
 
8. Return your completed form to D.S. Sewing, along with a photo of your pool.
*Note: you must leave the tape pieces around your pool until D.S. Sewing receives and approves your measurements.
